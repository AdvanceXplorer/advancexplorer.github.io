Research overview

My work sits at the intersection of stochastic optimal control, distribution steering, nonlinear trajectory optimization, uncertainty propagation, and autonomy. I am especially interested in methods that control not only one trajectory, but the evolution of uncertainty around trajectories.

Core themes

  • Distribution steering: steering probability laws under controlled stochastic dynamics.
  • Stochastic control: stochastic maximum principle, path-integral control, MPPI, and optimal stopping.
  • Astrodynamics: spacecraft guidance, re-entry, rendezvous, launch-vehicle GN&C, and uncertainty-aware mission design.
  • Learning dynamics: Koopman/Perron-Frobenius approximations, data-driven dynamics, policy evaluation, and V&V.

Design philosophy

My title, “Nothing is dense, everything is sparse, and uncertain,” reflects how I think about research: exploit sparse structure, respect uncertainty, and avoid pretending high-dimensional systems are simple dense objects.
