Stochastic optimal control

My stochastic control work combines stochastic calculus, stochastic maximum principle arguments, path-integral methods, MPPI, optimal stopping, and free-final-time conditions.

Representative directions

  • Fokker-Planck transformations of nonlinear stochastic optimal control problems.
  • Optimal stopping and common free-final-time transversality conditions.
  • Path-integral and MPPI algorithms using costate information, sparsity, and optimal transport.
  • Robust and adaptive control links to contraction metrics, H-infinity ideas, and nonlinear programming.

Why it matters

In aerospace and robotics, the controller should not merely track a nominal state. It should shape uncertainty, respond to stochastic disturbances, and exploit problem structure to remain computationally feasible.
