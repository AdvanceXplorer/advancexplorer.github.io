Learning Dynamics

Data-driven models, behavior-model evaluation, and robust validation for autonomy.

Learning dynamics and validation

I am interested in learning dynamics from data for control and autonomy while keeping validation, robustness, and interpretability at the center. This includes Koopman/Perron-Frobenius ideas, rollout analysis, and policy failure-mode evaluation.

Current interests

  • Data-driven dynamics models for control-oriented prediction and verification.
  • VLA and large behavior model evaluation through rollout analysis and failure modes.
  • Sparse and optimization-based interpretability for learned policies.
  • Validation/verification workflows for uncertain autonomy.

Connection to control

Learning is useful when it improves control decisions under uncertainty. I am especially interested in learned models that can be tested, stress-tested, interpreted, and connected back to stochastic control principles.
