Research topic

Distribution Steering

This topic studies how to control probability distributions under stochastic dynamics, with emphasis on covariance steering, Fokker–Planck formulations, free-final-time conditions, and nonlinear measure-valued dynamics.

Central question

How do we move uncertainty itself?

Instead of designing a single nominal trajectory, distribution steering asks for controls that shape the evolution of a full density, covariance, or non-Gaussian ensemble.

Current interests

Continuous-time covariance steering, common free-final time, stochastic maximum principle conditions, and turnpike behavior.

Nonlinear density control

Fokker–Planck transformations, optimal transport ideas, score/diffusion viewpoints, and computational approximations.

Applications

Spacecraft uncertainty propagation, autonomous guidance, re-entry dispersion management, and robust planning under uncertainty.
